Blue Lock Manga — Volume Summaries (1–37)

Volume 1

Yoichi Isagi’s football career begins with deep regret after his hesitation during a crucial high school match costs his team victory. Choosing to pass instead of shoot, Isagi realizes too late that his lack of ego denied him the chance to become a hero. This moment leaves him questioning whether he truly possesses the mindset required of a striker.

Everything changes when he receives an invitation to the Blue Lock project, a radical program created to revolutionize Japanese football. Inside the facility, Ego Jinpachi declares that Japan needs the world’s most selfish striker, setting the stage for a brutal competition where only one player can rise to the top.

Volume 2

Upon entering Blue Lock, Isagi is immediately thrown into a merciless survival game where players eliminate one another using soccer balls. Panic, fear, and instinct collide as participants realize their dreams can end at any moment.

Assigned to Team Z, the lowest-ranked team, Isagi begins observing the field rather than overpowering opponents. He slowly recognizes that his greatest strength lies in reading space and anticipating the flow of play.

Volume 3

Team Z struggles through early matches, suffering humiliating defeats that expose weaknesses and clashing egos. Strong personalities dominate while others falter under pressure.

During a crucial play, Isagi instinctively positions himself perfectly to score. This moment awakens his spatial awareness, revealing a weapon that could allow him to survive among elite strikers.

Volume 4

Barou Shoei’s overwhelming selfishness creates chaos within Team Z as he refuses to pass or cooperate. His raw dominance forces teammates to either adapt or be crushed.

Isagi learns to exploit Barou’s ego instead of opposing it, realizing that controlling others’ ambitions can be just as powerful as physical skill.

Volume 5

As the First Selection nears its conclusion, Team Z faces elimination in a must-win match. Pressure intensifies as fear threatens to break their resolve.

Isagi embraces responsibility and makes decisive plays, proving his evolution from a hesitant player into a striker capable of shaping the outcome.

Volume 6

The final match of the First Selection tests Team Z’s limits as every mistake could end their Blue Lock journey. Emotions run high as desperation fuels aggressive play.

Through adaptability and awareness, Isagi helps secure survival. He emerges stronger, understanding that ego is born through pressure.

Volume 7

The Second Selection begins, introducing smaller teams and harsher conditions. Players must now evolve rapidly or be left behind.

Isagi realizes that growth is not optional in Blue Lock — stagnation equals elimination.

Volume 8

Isagi partners with Nagi Seishiro, witnessing a level of natural talent beyond comprehension. Nagi’s effortless skill challenges Isagi’s self-worth.

Instead of despairing, Isagi studies Nagi’s movements, refining his own analytical playstyle to compensate for physical gaps.

Volume 9

Rin Itoshi appears as Blue Lock’s most dominant striker, controlling matches with overwhelming precision and calm.

Isagi becomes obsessed with surpassing Rin, marking the beginning of their intense rivalry.

Volume 10

Isagi closely observes Rin’s ability to dictate tempo and space, realizing the gap between them is mental as much as physical.

Determined to catch up, Isagi sharpens his decision-making and learns to remain composed under extreme pressure.

Volume 11

The Second Selection reaches its climax as players face elimination matches that test everything they have learned.

Isagi survives through rapid adaptation, proving he can evolve mid-game.

Volume 12

The surviving players form the Blue Lock Eleven, tasked with representing the project against Japan’s U-20 team.

This moment marks Blue Lock’s transition from experiment to national spotlight.

Volume 13

Blue Lock Manga — Volume Summaries (1–37)

Volume 1

Yoichi Isagi loses a crucial match due to hesitating to take a shot, leading him to doubt his instincts as a striker. He is invited to the Blue Lock project, a radical training program designed to create Japan’s ultimate egoist striker, where teamwork is immediately challenged.

Volume 2

Isagi enters Blue Lock and faces his first survival game, learning that elimination is permanent. He begins to understand Ego Jinpachi’s philosophy that a striker must prioritize scoring above all else.

Volume 3

The first selection continues as Team Z struggles, forcing Isagi to awaken his spatial awareness and realize his ability to read the field can become his weapon.

Volume 4

Isagi clashes with Barou’s overwhelming ego and begins learning that adapting to others’ strengths is key to survival within Blue Lock’s ruthless environment.

Volume 5

Team Z’s internal conflicts reach a peak, but Isagi steps up as a decisive force, accepting responsibility and proving that ego and teamwork can coexist when centered on winning.

Volume 6

The First Selection concludes, showing Isagi’s growth as a striker who can seize decisive moments, earning his place to advance deeper into Blue Lock.

Volume 7

The Second Selection begins, throwing players into intense 3v3 matches that emphasize stealing others’ abilities and evolving through direct competition.

Volume 8

Isagi teams up with Nagi and faces increasingly skilled opponents, learning that evolution requires abandoning comfort and embracing pressure.

Volume 9

Rin Itoshi is introduced as Blue Lock’s top player, setting up a powerful rivalry that challenges Isagi’s understanding of what it means to be a true striker.

Volume 10

Isagi begins chasing Rin’s level, sharpening his awareness and adaptability while realizing that true strength comes from dominating the flow of the game.

Volume 11

The Second Selection reaches its climax as players are pushed to their limits, with Isagi proving he can survive against overwhelming talent.

Volume 12

Preparations begin for the Blue Lock Eleven, shifting the story toward international recognition and professional-level competition.

Volume 13

The Blue Lock team faces Japan’s U-20 national team, symbolizing a clash between traditional football and Ego Jinpachi’s radical ideals.

Volume 14

The U-20 match intensifies, showcasing incredible plays and forcing each striker to prove their worth on a national stage.

Volume 15

Isagi makes a decisive impact in the U-20 match, marking a turning point that validates the Blue Lock project.

Volume 16

Volume 18

Volume 20

Volume 21

Rivalries intensify as players from different clubs clash, revealing contrasting philosophies of football.

Volume 22

Volume 23

Volume 28

Blue Lock Manga — Volume Summaries (1–37)

Volume 1

Yoichi Isagi’s football career begins with regret when his hesitation to shoot during a decisive high school match costs his team victory. Haunted by the realization that he lacked the ego to take responsibility, Isagi questions whether he truly has what it takes to be a striker who decides games.

His life changes when he receives an invitation to the Blue Lock project, a radical program designed to create Japan’s greatest striker. Inside the facility, Ego Jinpachi declares that teamwork has ruined Japanese football and that only a selfish goal scorer can lead Japan to victory.

Volume 2

Isagi enters Blue Lock and immediately faces a brutal survival game where players are eliminated without mercy. The fear of losing his future forces him to abandon hesitation and rely on instinct.

Assigned to Team Z, the weakest team, Isagi begins observing the movements of players around him. He realizes that understanding space and timing might allow him to survive against stronger opponents.

Volume 3

Team Z suffers crushing defeats, exposing internal conflicts and weaknesses. Strong personalities clash, and Isagi struggles to assert himself among dominant egos.

In a critical moment, Isagi instinctively positions himself to score, awakening his spatial awareness. This breakthrough confirms that his strength lies in reading the flow of the game.

Volume 4

Barou Shoei’s overwhelming ego disrupts Team Z’s coordination as he refuses to pass and plays solely for himself. His presence forces others to adapt or fail.

Isagi learns to exploit Barou’s selfishness instead of opposing it. This teaches him that understanding others’ egos is essential for survival.

Volume 5

Team Z faces a must-win match that will determine their survival in the First Selection. Pressure mounts as every mistake could end their dreams.

Isagi embraces responsibility and acts decisively, proving his growth as a striker capable of leading through ego and awareness.

Volume 6

The First Selection concludes with Team Z narrowly advancing. The experience leaves every player mentally hardened.

Isagi reflects on how far he has come, realizing he is no longer afraid to seize opportunities.

Volume 7

The Second Selection begins, forcing players into smaller teams and more intense matches.

Isagi learns that constant evolution is mandatory to survive this stage.

Volume 8
